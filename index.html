Set Goals. Be Present.

Summary

This app is designed to track and reduce screentime by creating a timer that tracks screen break times. This app serves anyone who is looking to be more present, procrastinate less, or simply use their phone less.

Problem

This addresses the user's fear of missing out and desire to improve. By reducing their screentime, the user is forming good habits and is more present.

Solution

This app offers rewards of the tribe and of the self. The user can connect with other users and gain praise through likes, and they get rewarded intrinsically for consistency and completion.

User Interviews

In my interviews with Abby, her main concern for a wellness app was screentime mitigation. She was looking to unplug for a set amount of time to focus on things like homework and mindfulness. In this case, the most useful thing would be an app that tracked and rewarded the user for a certain amount of time taken away from the phone. By rewarding the user for these behaviors, she would be more likely to focus on replacing bad tech habits with good ones.

Reflection

Challenges

  • In the beginning I struggled to keep the concept simple, and tried to involve too many actions and options in the interface.
  • Finding the right spacing between elements was difficult, because the screens looked different between the desktop and mobile XD apps.
  • I think that the app would have benefitted from more work on overlays and variable reward messages.

Successes

  • I think the overall visual design looks cohesive and simple.
  • I'm proud of the functionality of the prototype, I learned a lot about XD from this particular project.
  • I believe that the app adresses the user's needs in an easy and understandable.
